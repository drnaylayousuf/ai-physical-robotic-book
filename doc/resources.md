# Recommended Resources for Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

This page provides a curated list of resources to support your learning and development in Physical AI and humanoid robotics. These include books, papers, tools, communities, and educational materials.

## Books and Publications

### Core Textbooks
*   **"Robotics: Vision, Manipulation, and Control"** by S. Saha - Comprehensive coverage of fundamental robotics concepts
*   **"Probabilistic Robotics"** by S. Thrun, W. Burgard, and D. Fox - Essential for understanding uncertainty in robotics
*   **"Introduction to Autonomous Robots"** by G. Dudek and M. Jenkin - Great introduction to autonomous systems
*   **"Handbook of Robotics"** by B. Siciliano and O. Khatib - Comprehensive reference for all aspects of robotics
*   **"Robotics, Vision and Control"** by P. Corke - Practical approach with MATLAB examples

### AI and Machine Learning
*   **"Deep Learning"** by I. Goodfellow, Y. Bengio, and A. Courville - Comprehensive guide to deep learning
*   **"Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning"** by C. Bishop - Foundational text for ML concepts
*   **"Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach"** by S. Russell and P. Norvig - Classic AI textbook
*   **"Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction"** by R. Sutton and A. Barto - Essential for learning-based control

### Specialized Topics
*   **"Humanoid Robotics: A Reference"** by A. Goswami and M. Vidarsson - Comprehensive reference on humanoid robotics
*   **"The Cambridge Handbook of Humanoid Robotics"** by H. Park and J. Park - Academic reference
*   **"Human Walking"** by D. Winter - Understanding human locomotion for humanoid design

## Getting Started Pathways

### For Beginners
1. Start with basic programming (Python, C++)
2. Learn ROS 2 fundamentals through tutorials
3. Experiment with simulation environments (Gazebo, Webots)
4. Build simple projects with Arduino/Raspberry Pi
5. Progress to more complex robotics concepts

### For Researchers
1. Review foundational literature in your area of interest
2. Set up development environment with ROS 2
3. Reproduce results from key papers
4. Identify research gaps and opportunities
5. Develop novel approaches and validate experimentally

### For Practitioners
1. Identify specific application requirements
2. Select appropriate hardware and software platforms
3. Implement and test core capabilities
4. Integrate with existing systems
5. Validate in real-world scenarios
